📚 Understanding This Calculation
Which models and rulings this calculator uses
  • Nisab: 85g gold or 595g silver. Silver is used as the default threshold since it is lower — most scholars recommend it, as it brings more people into the obligation and channels more support to those who need it.
  • Rate: 2.5% (1/40) on qualifying wealth held for one full lunar year (ḥawl).
  • Gold & silver: valued at current market price, including jewellery, per the majority view.
  • Debts: Ḥanafī and Mālikī scholars deduct immediate, currently-due debts before calculating Zakat. Shāfiʿī and Ḥanbalī scholars generally count full assets regardless of debt. Use the toggle above to switch between them.
  • Shares & investments: short-term trading is zakated at full market value. Long-term holdings are only zakated on the company's underlying zakatable assets (cash, receivables, inventory) — commonly estimated at 25–33% of value if exact figures aren't available.
This is a simplified tool for estimation. For unusual assets, joint ownership, or business partnerships, ask your local scholar.
Who can receive Zakat (the 8 categories)

The Qur'an (9:60) specifies eight categories eligible to receive Zakat:

  1. Al-Fuqarā' — the poor
  2. Al-Masākīn — the needy
  3. Al-ʿĀmilīna ʿalayhā — those who administer and distribute Zakat
  4. Al-Mu'allafati Qulūbuhum — those whose hearts are being reconciled to Islam
  5. Fir-Riqāb — freeing people from bondage or captivity
  6. Al-Ghārimīn — those in debt they cannot repay
  7. Fī Sabīlillāh — in the path of Allah
  8. Ibn as-Sabīl — the stranded traveller
Zakat cannot go to your own parents, children, or spouse, since you are already responsible for their upkeep. Most scholars restrict it to Muslim recipients, with some allowing limited exceptions under Fī Sabīlillāh.
How to distribute your Zakat responsibly
  • Through your mahall committee — they know verified recipients locally and can distribute according to genuine need, which also strengthens community accountability.
  • Directly to eligible relatives outside your immediate dependents (e.g. siblings, cousins, extended family in need).
  • Verified Islamic welfare organizations that are transparent about where funds are spent.
  • Keep a simple record — date, amount, and recipient or organization — for your own accounting and peace of mind. Many mahalls now maintain digital Zakat registers for exactly this reason.
Zakat is generally best spent within your own locality first, unless there is a greater need elsewhere.
